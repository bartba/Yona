EQUIPMENT MANUAL — PCB Bonding Machine PCB Bonding Machine

Overview:
The PCB Bonding Machine mounts large and heavy ICs or connectors, up to 85 grams with a footprint up to 40 by 60 mm, onto a PCB and automatically applies epoxy adhesive around them to prevent parts from dislodging during transport. It uses a two-axis orthogonal servo robot with a travel range of 350 mm on the X-axis and 280 mm on the Y-axis, and a pneumatic dispenser head operating at 200 to 350 kPa. A 5-megapixel vision camera with a field of view of 30 by 30 mm detects fiducial marks on the PCB to an alignment accuracy of plus or minus 0.02 mm before dispensing. The dispenser needle is 22 gauge with an inner diameter of 0.41 mm, and each bond dot weighs approximately 0.05 grams. Nominal cycle time is 18 seconds per PCB.

Startup Procedure:
First, confirm that the air supply pressure at the regulator reads between 380 and 420 kPa. Then turn the main power breaker to ON and wait 10 seconds for the controller to fully boot. Press the servo-on button on the operation panel and confirm that all three servo drive indicator lights for the X-axis, Y-axis, and Z-axis turn green within 5 seconds. Next, press the home return button and wait for all axes to complete the home sequence — the display must show 0.00 mm on both the X and Y axes. Finally, verify that the ring light is on and the camera live image is visible on the monitor before switching to production mode.

Shutdown Procedure:
First, complete or cancel the current production cycle so the machine returns to the home position. Confirm the dispenser needle has retracted to the up position — the Z-axis display reads 0.00 mm. Press the servo-off button and wait for all three drive indicator lights to go dark. Then turn the main power breaker to OFF. Finally, close the air supply valve at the regulator; the pressure gauge should drop to 0 kPa within 30 seconds.

Servo Home Return Procedure:
On the controller touchscreen, navigate to the axis settings menu and select home return. The Y-axis moves to its mechanical end-stop at 20 mm per second, the controller sets the encoder origin there, then the axis retracts 5 mm to the software home position. The X-axis follows the same sequence immediately after. Total home return time is approximately 25 seconds. The display must show 0.00 mm on both axes before you can resume production.

Error 101 — Fiducial Mark Not Detected:
The vision camera failed to locate the PCB alignment reference marks. The acceptable detection contrast ratio is above 1.8; values below 1.5 trigger this error, typically caused by a dirty lens, insufficient ring light output, or the PCB being misaligned in the fixture. Clean the camera lens with a lint-free cloth, confirm the ring light is set to 80 percent output, reseat the PCB squarely within the locating pins, then restart the cycle. If the error repeats after two attempts, recalibrate the detection threshold in the vision settings menu.

Error 203 — Dispenser Pressure Out of Range:
The measured dispensing pressure fell outside the normal operating range of 200 to 350 kPa. A pressure below 180 kPa typically indicates a clogged needle or a cartridge with less than 10 ml of bond remaining; a pressure above 370 kPa indicates a blocked outlet or an incorrect setpoint. Replace the 22-gauge needle if the inner bore shows adhesive buildup, verify the cartridge weight on the load cell reads above 12 grams, then confirm the pressure regulator is set to 280 kPa and perform a 3-second purge before restarting.

Error 315 — Servo Position Deviation Exceeded:
The servo controller detected that the actual axis position differs from the commanded position by more than 0.05 mm, which is the allowable tracking error threshold. This typically results from a mechanical obstruction, a loose motor coupling whose set screws should be torqued to 1.2 newton-meters, or a degraded encoder cable with signal amplitude below 0.8 volts peak-to-peak. Remove any obstruction, check coupling tightness, then perform the full home return sequence before resuming production.

Error 422 — Bond Level Low:
The load cell below the cartridge holder measured a weight below 12 grams, corresponding to approximately 10 ml of remaining adhesive. Remove the empty cartridge, install a new 30-ml cartridge, then run the purge cycle three times — each purge lasts 2 seconds — to eliminate air pockets before resuming.

Error 508 — Emergency Stop Activated:
The machine stopped due to a safety interlock. The safety door switch triggers this error when the door gap exceeds 3 mm, and the E-stop button or foreign-object sensor can also cause it. Ensure the safety door is fully closed and latched, release the E-stop mushroom button by rotating it clockwise until it clicks out, verify by visual inspection that no objects are inside the work envelope, then press the reset button on the panel to re-enable the servos and resume.

Emergency Alarm Recovery Procedure:
When any emergency alarm occurs and the machine has halted, follow this three-stage recovery sequence. First, alarm reset: on the controller touchscreen, navigate to the alarm log screen, confirm the alarm code and cause, then press the alarm reset button — the alarm indicator light on the panel will go from red to off. Second, equipment reset: press the system reset button to reinitialize all servo drives and I/O modules; the controller will perform a self-check lasting approximately 8 seconds, and all axis status indicators must return to green before proceeding. Third, restart: press the home return button to run the full origin sequence, confirm both axes read 0.00 mm, then select the production program and press the cycle start button. If the alarm reactivates within the first three cycles, do not attempt further resets and contact the equipment manager — in Korean say 담당자 김상식 내선 이삼일칠번, in English say Kim Sangsik at extension 2317.
